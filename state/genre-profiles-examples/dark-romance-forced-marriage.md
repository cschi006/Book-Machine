## 1. Positioning (the business)

- **Subgenre:** dark contemporary romance — **arranged / forced marriage** core
- **Comps (illustrative — confirm current):** Rina Kent; Ana Huang (*Twisted* series); Sophie Lark (*Brutal Birthright*)
- **Target reader & platform:** KU dark-romance / BookTok reader; binge-reader; indie self-pub
- **Target word count:** 80–90k · **Series/standalone:** standalone (world could extend later)
- **The fantasy it sells:** *forced to marry the dangerous man — and the danger becomes devotion.*
- **Tone:** intense, morally-grey, escalating, claustrophobic-then-tender

## 2. The genre promise & deal-breakers

- **Reader promise:** a morally-grey, possessive hero; a forced bond that turns real; high heat; an earned HEA.
- **Mandatory payoffs (non-negotiable):** **HEA** (hard — guaranteed this book); the forced-marriage bond paying off as genuine devotion; the hero's darkness *aimed at protecting her*.
- **Deal-breakers (one-star triggers):** no HEA; **ambush content** (missing content warnings); cheating *between the leads*; a hero whose cruelty is never reframed/earned.

## 3. Heat & content

- **Heat level:** **explicit / high** — multiple on-page explicit scenes.
- **Content stance:** dark/taboo dynamics **embraced but INTERROGATED** — the narrative is aware of the power imbalance and examines it; not endorsed blindly.
- **Content warnings (front matter, required):** captivity/control dynamics, explicit content, violence; finalize per actual content.

## 4. Beat contract (what trope-promise checks against)

| # | Beat | ~% | Must land |
|---|------|----|-----------|
| 1 | The deal/decree that forces the marriage | 3–6% | the trap snaps shut; no easy out |
| 2 | First collision as bound parties | 10% | antagonism + undeniable pull |
| 3 | Forced proximity escalates | 20–35% | control vs. defiance; cracks of humanity in him |
| 4 | Midpoint: the bond turns real | ~50% | a truth/act that flips antagonism → want (interrogates the dynamic) |
| 5 | Intimacy + external threat both rise | 55–70% | high heat; the danger that justifies his darkness closes in |
| 6 | Dark moment / betrayal-shaped break | ~75% | driven by his wound/her autonomy — not contrivance |
| 7 | Grovel / proven change | 85–92% | his darkness re-aimed to protect *her* choice, by ACTION |
| 8 | Hard HEA | 96–99% | the forced bond chosen freely; safety + devotion |

- **Promised tropes:** forced/arranged marriage (primary), morally-grey possessive hero, forced proximity, touch-her-and-die protectiveness.

## 5. Pacing & structure norms

- **Tension curve:** fast claustrophobic rise → midpoint flip → dark-moment trough ~75% → climax peak.
- **Hook speed:** the forcing event on/near page 1.
- **Chapter length:** 2.5–4k · **Scenes/chapter:** 1–2 · **Total chapters (est.):** ~24–30 for 80–90k.

## 6. Voice, POV & prose register

- **POV/tense:** **dual 1st-person**, present or past (genre norm; alternating his/hers).
- **Register:** commercial-propulsive, emotionally immediate, deep-POV — NOT literary.
- **Interiority:** deep, obsessive on his side; defiant/observant on hers. **Dialogue:** balanced, charged, subtext-heavy.

## 7. Meter tolerances (genre-adjusted)

- **Keep as convention, NOT crutch:** possessive/obsessive diction ("*mine*," ownership language), heartbeat/breath spikes in high-heat beats, repeated power-dynamic motifs — these are load-bearing. Ration only true overuse.
- **Cliché vs trope:** "enemies who can't keep their hands off," the dangerous-man-gentle-only-with-her — these are PROMISES to deliver, not clichés to cut.

## 8. Per-agent calibration

| Player | Baseline for this book |
|--------|------------------------|
| trope-promise | Beats = §4; forced-marriage + morally-grey hero delivered with signature beats; HEA mandatory (§2) |
| pacing-whole / passage | Curve = §5; dark moment ~75%, fast claustrophobic rise; chapters 2.5–4k |
| payoff | HEA + the bond-becomes-real + protective-darkness payoffs are non-negotiable |
| adjudicator | Acquiring bar = KU dark-romance editor; judge commercial pull + does the hero earn it |
| audience | Hook = the forcing event p.1; what must land = the pull by §4 midpoint |
| line-editor / voice / concertmaster | Register §6 (propulsive, not literary); KEEP §7 possessive/obsessive convention |
| dialogue-polish | Charged, subtext-heavy, power-laden banter; he controls, she defies |
| **sensitivity** | Content line = §3: dark dynamics **interrogated, hard HEA, full CWs**; flag if cruelty is endorsed-not-examined, or consent is hand-waved as romantic; recommend a human reader for the heaviest beats |
| ai-decontamination | Apply §7 — don't strip possessive/obsessive diction as if it were a crutch |
| soloist | Draft to §3 explicit heat, §5 pacing, §6 dual-1st register |
| score-architect / world-builder / casting-director | Structure = §4; hero/heroine wounds serve the §1 fantasy |

## 9. Market notes (Scout research, 2025–26)

- **Trends:** dark romance going mainstream in 2026; "maximalist" appetite (darkest/most intense). Mafia + age gap + captive + alternating POV dominate the top-20; **arranged/forced marriage is among the hottest tropes.** Gothic-slasher & dark classic reimaginings rising.
- **Cover/blurb:** dark moody covers, object/symbol or faceless-couple; blurb leads with the forced bond + the danger; CWs near the buy.
