## The Ledger

Every recurring thread in the book lives in one table, whatever its kind.

**Type** — what kind of thread:
- `PROMISE` — a question/expectation raised in the reader's mind
- `GAG` — a running joke
- `MOTIF` — a recurring image, phrase, or symbol
- `DEVICE` — a planted object (a Chekhov's gun)
- `CALLBACK` — a line or beat meant to echo a specific earlier moment
- `ARC` — a character-change beat that spans movements

**Status** — where the thread is in its life:
- `OPEN` — raised, not yet developed
- `SEEDED` — planted in an early movement, waiting
- `ESCALATING` — building across movements toward its payoff
- `PAID` — landed
- `OPEN-BY-DESIGN` — deliberately left open (e.g. a series hook)

**Touchpoints** — *the column the backward wave runs on.* List **every movement this thread appears in or must appear in** (e.g. `2, 9, 20`). When a new movement changes a thread, the Conductor wakes exactly the players in this list — no one else.
