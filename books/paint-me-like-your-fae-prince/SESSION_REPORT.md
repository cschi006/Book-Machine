## Voice Anchor — Quick Reference (the most reusable artifact)

The full anchor lives in `state/voice-anchor.md`. Short version:

### Bianca

- **Register:** Casual blogger, internet-native, lowercase *ok* and *wtf*, em-dashes and ellipses natural. Anglo-Saxon over Latinate.
- **Body-first stress:** My stomach hurt. My palms were sweating. My legs had opinions.
- **Pop refs:** Short, modern, one-line — *Mona Lisa expression*, *bad layer comp in Photoshop*, *HR meetings*. Never extended.
- **Hospitality reflex:** Even under threat, she brings water/tea/pillow. Load-bearing character beat.
- **Tactical inventory lists when overwhelmed:** *Corridor. Two guards. One direction forward.*
- **Anaphoric inventory when overwhelmed (Signature Move 7):** *The contents of my business were... The contents of my business were... The contents of my business were...*
- **Naked admission single-sentence paragraphs:** *Like all the others, I had no memory of making it.*
- **Self-talk out loud when alone:** *"What would Mara do?"*
- **Never:** filter words (felt/noticed/realized), weather opens, extended literary metaphor during threat, narrator domestication of strangeness.

### Felix

- **Register:** Elevated, modern formal English. Not archaic. No medieval grandiosity.
- **Visible body emotion:** *I sighed. I huffed. I chuckled. I sat seething.* Verbs surface, never compressed-to-invisible.
- **Diminutive interior labels for Bianca:** *the small angry mortal, the anxious human* — eroded across the manuscript as relationship axis climbs. By Ch 14 he just thinks *Bianca.*
- **Short declarative dialogue:** *Open it. Show me. No.*
- **Cai banter:** dry, warm, idiom-sharing (*you catch more pixies with nectar than a silver net*). The only place his humor surfaces.
- **Court hierarchy in vocabulary:** *my court standards, royal armory, Prince Felix, King Idris.*
- **Italicized one-word interior reactions:** *Seizure? Normal. Nectar it is then.*
- **Punch-line chapter endings:** set up → reverse → land, cut at the revelation.
- **Assessment-stacking under pressure (Signature Move 7):** *The first calculation was political. The second... The third...*
- **Never:** archaic diction, compressed-to-invisible emotion, over-analytical processing past the revelation point.

### Both POVs

- **Voice Check Question (single test):** *Does this passage stay inside the POV character's body and immediate scoring — in her specific register, without filter words, backstory padding, or domestication of threat?*
