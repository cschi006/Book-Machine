## The Reader Promise

> What is the reader buying? Three load-bearing promises, derived from the dossier's themes and confirmed by author.

**Promise 1:** *The introvert heroine done correctly.* Bianca's social anxiety is accommodated, not cured. She finds presence through chosen vulnerability, not extroversion. By the end she is still anxious and still introverted — she has stopped using distance as architecture.

**Promise 2:** *The fae prince's control is load-bearing.* Felix's authority is real and his trust is built, not declared. He does not soften all at once. His final choice — to live a mortal lifespan with Bianca — costs him a real future.

**Promise 3:** *The magic system's cost lands as concrete, not cool.* True names are not décor; their dilution is a measurable wound. Cai's diminishment is a visible counter throughout the book.

---

## The Core Wound / Central Conflict

**Protagonist wound:** Bianca's gift activated unbidden in adolescence. She could not explain it, learned to hide it, and built a life designed for non-exposure. Being seen completely is her fear and her need.

**Antagonist or counter-force:** Evander — Felix's exiled uncle, operating in the mortal world as Mr. Terry. He has been targeting Bianca's Etsy shop for 18 months, building a true-name arsenal against everyone who voted for his exile.

**Core question of the book:** Can presence survive once distance stops protecting anyone?
