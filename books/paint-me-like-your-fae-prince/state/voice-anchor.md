## Voice Fingerprint — Shared

This is a dual first-person past-tense paranormal romance with two distinct POV voices that share three load-bearing rules: emotion is rendered through the body and not named directly, prose stays inside the POV character's immediate experience without omniscient leakage, and the camera never drifts into atmospheric or literary mode during a tense beat. Both voices score — they observe, register, decide — rather than describing from a remove. Filter words (felt, noticed, realized) are nearly absent and should stay that way. Both voices admit things plainly: when they understand something, they say it, in one sentence, often as a single-line paragraph. The genre is commercial paranormal romance for a KU audience; when craft instinct and commercial directness conflict, commercial wins.

---

## Voice Fingerprint — Bianca

Bianca's voice is a working-artist's voice — a small-business owner with social anxiety who lives most of her life through screens and knows her own friction points. She narrates her body directly and without softening: *my stomach hurt, my palms were sweating, my own heartbeat was audible in my ears.* She does not say she is afraid; she reports what her body is doing while she's afraid. She uses anger as a deliberate engineering tool: *with every step I made myself angrier.* She talks to herself out loud when alone. She runs tactical exit lists when overwhelmed. She uses contemporary platform-specific vocabulary (Etsy, Instagram, Ring, Slack, Photoshop) and casual register markers (*ok*, *wtf*, ellipses-as-trailing-thought, em-dashes used naturally). Her pop culture references are short and modern — never extended metaphors. She is wry but not literary; her cleverness operates at sentence length, not paragraph length.

## Voice Fingerprint — Felix

Felix's voice is a prince-in-the-field voice — elevated but not archaic, controlled but not buried. His internal emotions surface in his body (*I sighed. I huffed. I chuckled. I sat seething*) rather than being compressed out of existence. He categorizes mortals dismissively in his interior (*the small angry mortal, this idiot woman*) but listens when Cai checks him. He speaks formally in declaratives — *Open it. Show me. No.* — and lets explanations stay tight. Court hierarchy is in his vocabulary (*my court standards, royal armory, Prince Felix, King Idris*). His dry humor is reserved almost entirely for Cai, with whom he shares idioms (*you catch more pixies with nectar than a silver net*). The dryness almost never surfaces toward Bianca in dialogue, but it appears in his interior asides when she surprises him (*Nectar it is then.*). His chapters end on a punch — set up, reverse, land — and stop at the moment of revelation, not the analysis after it.

## Sentence Rhythm Profile

### Bianca

- **Typical sentence length:** 8–18 words in action and dialogue; 18–35 in observation paragraphs that wind through inventory.
- **Fragment use:** Yes, at emotional peaks and admissions. *Good. That would have to do. Like all the others, I had no memory of making it.*
- **Paragraph length:** 1–4 sentences typically. Single-sentence paragraphs at moments of admission, recognition, or scoring. New paragraph for every dialogue beat.
- **Rhythm signature:** Punchy three-beat openings (*Neither detail was ideal. Like all the others. Good.*), then a longer winding sentence when she's processing, then a clipped declarative when she lands.

### Felix

- **Typical sentence length:** 8–20 words. Cleaner and more deliberate than Bianca.
- **Fragment use:** Sparingly, often as single-word italicized interior reactions. *Seizure? Normal. Nectar it is then.*
- **Paragraph length:** 2–4 sentences typically. Compact. He scores, decides, moves on.
- **Rhythm signature:** Short declarative response in dialogue, then a mid-length assessment in interior. Punch-line endings at chapter close.

## Vocabulary Register

### Bianca

- **Level:** Grounded, casual, contemporary. Anglo-Saxon vocabulary preferred over Latinate.
- **Word texture:** Physical and specific. Domestic detail and platform-specific nouns over abstract or atmospheric ones.
- **Image pool:** Suburban-domestic (field hockey stick from college, mourning dove alarm, padded mailers), digital-native (Photoshop layer comp, Slack notifications, Ring feed), small-business-owner (Etsy, Instagram, packing tape, label printer).
- **Register breaks she does:** Casual markers — *ok* (lowercase), *wtf*, *fuck*, *bazillion*, *unhinged*, *Jesus Christ* in dialogue.
- **Register breaks she avoids:** No literary synonyms when the simple word works. No purple description. No extended metaphor at high stakes.

### Felix

- **Level:** Elevated and precise, but not archaic. Modern formal English.
- **Word texture:** Assessment language. Latinate when assessment requires it (*confirmation, containment, accommodation, glamour*), Anglo-Saxon for action.
- **Image pool:** Court life and statecraft (*royal armory, court standards, magical infrastructure*), tactical assessment, the body of his attendant and the body of the mortal.
- **Register breaks he does:** Italicized one-word interior asides. Occasional formal courtesy in dialogue (*Of course. Indeed.*). Contractions present but fewer than Bianca uses.
- **Register breaks he avoids:** No archaic vocabulary. No "thee/thou" pseudo-fantasy diction. No medieval flourishes. No fantasy-generic grandiosity.

## Signature Moves

*These are hers and his. The Driver should look for opportunities to use them.*

### Bianca's signature moves

1. **The naked-admission single-sentence paragraph.**
   > *Like all the others, I had no memory of making it.*
   > Use when: Bianca reaches a moment of understanding or self-disclosure. The whole admission goes in one sentence and gets its own paragraph. No softening clauses.

2. **Body-as-its-own-character reports.**
   > *My stomach was doing something unhelpful. My legs knew what was waiting at the end of the front walk and had opinions about it that I was choosing not to entertain.*
   > Use when: anxiety is climbing. Her body becomes a third party she's negotiating with. Two-to-three short sentences in sequence. No "I felt."

3. **Tactical inventory lists when overwhelmed.**
   > *Close door, lock it, call someone. Yell, create witnesses. Hopefully Mr. Terry had his hearing aids already on. Swing hockey stick if it came to that.*
   > Use when: she is split between conversation and exit math. Comma-separated short clauses. The list is the visible evidence of her brain doing two jobs at once.

4. **Self-talk out loud.**
   > *"What would Mara do?" I said, trying to calm myself down.*
   > Use when: she is alone or functionally alone and needs to manufacture courage. The tag explains what she's doing. Mara is a frequent invocation.

5. **Short pop-culture reference as quick aside.**
   > *Mona Lisa expression. Bad layer comp in Photoshop. Resting bitch face.*
   > Use when: she needs to land a comparison fast. One image, one line, no extension. Modern and platform-specific.

6. **"That would have to do" — the working-with-what-she-has acceptance line.**
   > *That would have to do.*
   > Use sparingly, at moments where she chooses to act with imperfect equipment.

7. **Object-focused anaphoric observation when overwhelmed.**
   > *The contents of the studio that Felix could see were not the contents of my business. The contents of my business were a four-year mailing list... The contents of my business were not in this house. The contents of my business were somewhere between here and the recipient's mailbox...*
   > Use when: Bianca's mind is processing the scale of something her body can't yet hold. The repeated *The [thing]* sentence-opener produces a list-as-rhetoric effect. **Confirmed voice 2026-05-26** after Voice Steward investigation on Laps 2–3 — `The` opener density 25–28% is voice, not crutch, when used as inventory-under-pressure. Anchor's `The`-opener soft-watch is now scene-specific: flag only if it appears outside an overwhelm beat.

### Felix's signature moves

1. **Visible-body interior emotion.**
   > *I huffed. I sighed. I chuckled. I sat seething.*
   > Use when: an emotion surfaces. Felix's feelings live in single short verbs, not in compressed-out interior monologue.

2. **The diminutive interior label for Bianca.**
   > *the small angry mortal. this idiot woman. the anxious human.*
   > Use when: Bianca is doing something Felix finds inconvenient. He categorizes her dismissively in interior — never in dialogue. The label is his control mechanism.

3. **Cai-Felix banter as warm idiom-sharing.**
   > *"If you wanted to try out a mortal couch that badly, I would have gotten us a hotel room." / "Prince Felix, you catch more pixies with nectar than a silver net."*
   > Use when: they're alone or near-alone. They tease. They share fae idioms. Cai checks him; he listens.

4. **Short declarative commands in dialogue.**
   > *Open it. Show me. No.*
   > Use when: Felix is asserting authority. One-word or two-word sentences. He does not explain himself when commanding.

5. **The punch-line chapter ending.**
   > *I came here believing I would find the mortal with information about the breach to our world. Instead, I found the breach to our world in the mortal.*
   > Use when: closing a Felix chapter. Set up the assumption, reverse it, land. End the chapter at the reversal — not the analysis after.

6. **Italicized one-word interior reaction.**
   > *Seizure? Normal. Nectar it is then.*
   > Use when: something Bianca says or does lands sideways with him. The italicized fragment is his uncensored reaction before the assessment kicks in.

7. **Assessment-stacking under pressure.**
   > *The first calculation was political. The second calculation was the one I did not let move my face. The third was procedural.*
   > Use when: Felix is recalibrating in real time after a revelation. He thinks in numbered, parallel-structured categories — first/second/third or named units (*political, internal, procedural*). Same `The`-opener density as Bianca's Signature Move 7 but a different mechanism: his is architectural cataloging of options, hers is overwhelm anaphora. Voice Steward should distinguish: stacking = voice; clustered descriptive *The* in non-stress contexts = potential crutch. **Confirmed voice 2026-05-26 after Lap 4 audit.**

## What These Voices Never Do

*These are voice rules, not craft opinions. The Driver does not do these.*

### Both POVs never:

- Use filter words: *felt, noticed, realized, seemed, appeared, watched as.* Direct sensation only.
- Name an emotion before showing it in the body. *She was afraid* is banned; *her mouth went dry* is the move.
- Drift into omniscient or atmospheric description during a tense beat.
- Explain backstory in the opening paragraphs of a scene. Drop the reader in. Trust the strangeness.
- Use AI-default constructions: *something in his X, she couldn't help [gerund], her heart [verb], the air between them [verb], she found herself [gerund].*

### Bianca never:

- Opens a scene with weather or atmospheric description.
- Reaches for extended literary metaphor during a high-threat beat. Her wit is sentence-length, not paragraph-length, and it goes quiet when she's actively running exits. *(Cross-reference: voice-self-edit pattern 3.)*
- Domesticates the strange thing with a quip when the scene needs the strangeness to land. Her humor is fine after a threat passes; it must not deflate the threat in real time. *(Cross-reference: voice-self-edit pattern 7.)*
- Explains the dissociative-amnesia backstory beyond the minimum the scene requires. She has a private vocabulary for what's happening to her; the reader gets only what serves the scene. *(Cross-reference: voice-self-edit pattern 1.)*
- Stops scoring. She is always tracking — Felix's tactical errors, Cai's accommodations, her own body's state. When she notices an antagonist's weakness, she banks it. *(Cross-reference: voice-self-edit pattern 5.)*

### Felix never:

- Uses archaic or medieval diction. No *thee, thou, mine own, mayhap, perchance*. No fantasy-generic grandiosity.
- Compresses emotion to invisibility. His feelings surface in the body. Buried compression is wrong for this Felix.
- Continues analytical processing past the moment of understanding. End the scene at the revelation, cut the assessment. *(Cross-reference: voice-self-edit pattern 6.)*
- Softens his contempt for Bianca's mortal concerns in interior. The contempt is part of his control. It can erode across the book — but it does not vanish in chapter 2.
- Breaks the banter dynamic with Cai. With Cai he is dry, warm, and called out. That is the relationship.
