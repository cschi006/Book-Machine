## Reader Promise Contract

*(load-bearing promises from book-order.md)*

1. **The introvert heroine done correctly** — Bianca's social anxiety is accommodated, not cured. She finds presence through chosen vulnerability, not extroversion. By the end she is still anxious and still introverted, and has stopped using distance as architecture.

2. **The fae prince's control is load-bearing** — Felix's authority is real, his trust is built, not declared. He does not soften all at once. His final choice — to live a mortal lifespan with Bianca — costs him a real future.

3. **The magic system's cost lands as concrete, not cool** — True names are not décor. Cai's diminishment is the visible counter throughout the book. Every recovery is felt, every loss is paid for.
