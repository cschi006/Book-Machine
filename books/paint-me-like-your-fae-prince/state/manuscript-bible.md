## World Rules

**Setting:** Contemporary mortal world (suburban Atlanta) + Loren, a fae realm accessible by portal. Time moves differently between the realms but the story does not lean on the difference.

**Time period / era:** Present day (2026). Late summer / early autumn timeframe.

**Physical rules (magic — true-name system):**

1. **True Name = Source of Power.** A fae's true name is the literal source of their magical power. It is reserved for life-or-death moments. Casual use is unthinkable.
2. **Dilution by Knowledge.** The more people who know a fae's true name AND have seen their face, the more diluted the fae's power becomes. A widely-known name can render a fae functionally powerless and, if diluted far enough, extinguish them entirely.
3. **The Shield Rule.** If a fae physically meets someone holding their true name (via active original portrait), the fae cannot use magic to harm that person. Absolute. Load-bearing.
4. **The Compulsion.** Beyond the shield, the named fae is involuntarily drawn toward the holder — trust, warmth, favorable judgment. Strengthens with contact. Cannot be willed away. Politically catastrophic at court.
5. **Hypnagogic Channel (Bianca's gift).** Activates in the half-sleep state between waking and sleeping. Her hand moves before she is fully conscious. She perceives the fae realm and named fae through this channel; she does not invent. Only physical originals carry the link — digital reproductions are inert. Burning the original breaks that specific link AND erases the exact name/likeness from memory (preserved only if recorded inert elsewhere).
6. **Conscious art is separate.** Bianca's normal graphic design and conscious illustration carry no link. Only half-sleep originals do.

**Social rules / power structures:**
- **Loren court:** Aristocratic. King Idris is currently weakened; Felix functions as ruler in all but title. Vael is Felix's second-in-command (institutional loyalty). Cai is Felix's personal attendant (chosen-friend loyalty).
- **Mortal world:** Ordinary. Bianca operates a small-business artist/graphic-designer life. Mara is her human-world anchor.
- **Exile politics:** Evander was exiled by Idris years ago for murdering Felix's mortal mother Anwyll. Stripped of rank, magic, and timelessness. Operating on mortal time turned grievance into obsession.

**Geography (key locations):**
- **Bianca's townhouse, suburban Atlanta:** Two-floor townhouse. Studio upstairs. Living room, kitchen downstairs. Front porch. Field hockey stick in front hall. Ring camera. Townhouse row with shared fence.
- **Mr. Terry's townhouse:** Adjacent. Same floor plan as Bianca's. Older retiree appearance. (Reader to learn later: Evander's base of operations, with fae-warded basement.)
- **Loren — corridor:** Carved ribs across ceiling. Pale veining in floor catching light like something moving under ice. Narrow windows cut too high to see through. Empty plinth in left alcove. Right wall bows inward slightly. Bianca has been drawing this corridor since age 13.
- **Loren — strategy chamber:** Smaller. Long narrow stone table. Two chairs (not six). Low sideboard with pitcher, cups, basin. One plain wall.

## Sensory Vocabulary

> Words and images that feel right for this book. Drawn from dossier sensory palette + author samples.

**Textures:** Cold carved stone, smooth ink-warm paper, sticky-ink fingertips, archival sleeves, glassine, padded mailers, Bristol paper, the give of a worn field hockey stick handle.

**Sounds:** Static-hush (the air-quality just before magic resolves), the small mechanical click of a portal closing, Ring camera notification chirp, label printer whine, soft mourning-dove alarm, fluorescent buzz.

**Smells:** Hot ink under a desk lamp, dried graphite, archival glue, the dust of a long-undisturbed basement (Evander's, later), warm electronics, the chemical exhaust of a neighbor's truck warming up.

**Light qualities:** Light filtered through water or glass even indoors (Loren signature). Lamps that burn into "a small white sun behind the eyes." Bald floodlight from driveway sensors. Late-summer Atlanta evening sky bruised blue until almost nine.

**Recurring motifs:** Ink, paper, the uncanny precision of fae beauty (faces too symmetrical), the static-hush before workings, glamour edges that fail at the mouth or temple, the act of being watched or recognized.
