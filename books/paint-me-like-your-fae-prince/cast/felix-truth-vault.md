## Identity

**Full name:** Felix, Prince of Loren. (True name reserved per magic system — revealed only in Ch 15.)
**Role in story:** Co-lead, romantic interest, alternating POV
**POV character?** Yes — Chapters 2, 5, 7, 9, 11, 13
**Age:** Equivalent of early-to-mid thirties; actual fae age older. The text does not linger on the number.
**Physical:** The kind of beautiful that reads slightly wrong to human eyes — too symmetrical, too precise, the way a perfectly rendered illustration is uncanny next to a photograph. Holds himself like someone who has spent decades being the most dangerous person in the room and learned to look relaxed about it. His public face is completely assembled. The seams are not visible unless you know where to look.

---

## The Wound

> Authority before personhood. He learned to lead from behind a wall, and the wall became structural.

**What happened:** Fifteen years ago, King Idris began declining. Felix took on the full work of ruling without the title. Bianca has been sketching Idris since age 13 — Felix did not know this until the Loren corridor on the other side of Bianca's portal told him the breach was older than tonight. The court treats him as king in all but name. He was given authority before he was given time to be known.

**Who was there:** Idris, weakening. Cai, the only person who saw him before the role hardened. The court, who needed a functional ruler and got one.

**What he did or failed to do:** He filled the empty space with work and never revisited it. He did not let anyone in close enough to see what duty cost him.

**What he still feels responsible for:** Cai's diminishment — every active portrait of his closest friend is a wound Felix failed to find soon enough. Anwyll's death — he was old enough to understand his mother's murder shaped his father's reign and his own boyhood.

**The sensory anchor:** The particular cold of Loren stone when he stops moving long enough to feel it. The look his father had when Anwyll died. The small slip in Cai's glamour at the mouth — the place the diminishment shows first.

---

## The Mask

> The Prince. Controlled, politically precise, unnervingly calm.

**Default mask:** Demanding and used to being obeyed. Does not soften his edges. Does not apologize for his directness. Not a performance — it is a real part of him. But it is not all of him.

**What cracks the mask:** Cai in real distress (Ch 1 — his composure visibly breaks). Bianca's refusal to fear him correctly. Cai's idiom-banter that he can't help responding to. Bianca's hospitality — the water-and-tea routine he did not know mortals could do under threat.

**What he does when the mask cracks:** Goes more compressed, not less. His control narrows rather than loosens. Smirks. Stillness. The people who know him well read this correctly as danger. Bianca reads it as him being insufferable.

---

## The Operating System

**Core belief about himself:** I have been alone so long I am no longer sure I know how to be otherwise. Being truly known would cost me the authority my people rely on.

**Core belief about other people:** Loyalty must be measured by action, restraint, and public risk when competing obligations collide. Compulsion can mimic warmth. Trust must be earned, never declared.

**What he will never do again:** Let a weakness become visible to enemies. (The court must not see Cai's diminishment, his father's decline, or his own fixation on Bianca.)

**What he desperately wants but won't admit:** To be known by someone who has absolutely no reason to pretend and enough nerve to look directly at him.

---

## Body Memory

**Under stress:** He smirks. He does not pace, shout, or retreat. He goes quiet, narrows his focus, and becomes even more precise. His emotions surface in his body — *I huffed. I sighed. I chuckled. I sat seething.* The verb is small but visible.

**When lying or strategizing:** Categorizes the target dismissively in interior (*the small angry mortal, this idiot woman, the anxious human*). The diminutive label is his control mechanism, not a literal assessment of her.

**When safe / relaxed (with Cai):** Banter. Idiom-sharing (*you catch more pixies with nectar than a silver net*). Lets Cai check him. Dry humor that almost never surfaces with anyone else.

**Signature physical gesture:** Two fingers laid beside a piece of evidence rather than on it. Setting his hand on a tabletop to organize the conversation around it. Hands loose on his knees when seated — never crossed, never gripping.

## Private Vocabulary

- *Containment* — what he is trying to do at all times
- *The breach* — his term for whatever destabilizes the court (mortal, magical, or political)
- *The shape of the threat* — what he constructs after assessment
- Court titles he uses: *Prince Felix, King Idris, my Prince, second*
- Fae idioms with Cai: *nectar / silver net*, others to develop
- *The map*, *the route*, *the brief* — operational language for missions
- Italicized one-word interior reactions: *Seizure? Normal. Nectar it is then.*
