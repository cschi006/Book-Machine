## Identity

**Full name:** Bianca Lawson
**Role in story:** Lead protagonist, primary POV
**POV character?** Yes — Chapters 1, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15
**Age:** 29
**Physical:** Unremarkably pretty in a way she actively works against noticing. Average height, tends toward stillness — the kind of person who holds herself still so no one looks twice. Dark circles from irregular sleep cycles. Ink-stained fingers she never quite gets clean. Carries herself slightly inward, like she is always prepared to take up less space than she actually needs.

---

## The Wound

> The gift activated unbidden in adolescence. She could not control it. She did not know what it was. She learned to hide it.

**What happened:** Around age 13, she began waking with finished portraits of strangers on her desk — work she did not remember making, far beyond her conscious skill level. She named the subjects. She invented backstories. She had no vocabulary for what was happening to her. She tried, once, to mention it to her doctor. The result was a label — dissociative amnesia — and a private decision: this would never come out again.

**Who was there:** No one, by design. Her parents thought she was "just gifted." She made sure that was the only available explanation.

**What she did or failed to do:** She built her entire life around not being seen this way. Remote work. Etsy shop. Tight friend circle. The drawings became a private eccentricity she sold as "fantasy art" — she thought she was building a fictional world.

**What she still feels responsible for:** Every moment she has been unwittingly producing real true-name weapons for fourteen years. The harm done while she thought she was just being weird is not abstract to her once she sees Cai diminished.

**The sensory anchor** *(what she still smells/hears/sees when it surfaces):* Hot ink under a desk lamp. The taste of having drooled on paper. The particular static-hush that arrives a beat before her body locks down. The feeling of waking up with two fingers stuck together and not remembering why.

## The Mask

> Competence.

**Default mask:** Online, she is charming, warm, and fully herself — she has designed engagement that operates through a screen at a safe remove. In person, she is efficient, slightly clipped, and very good at appearing fine. She does not let people see the machinery. The mask is not dishonest — she IS competent. But competence is the part she shows so the rest stays hidden.

**What cracks the mask:** Direct concern from Mara that she can't deflect. Cai's accurate read of her social state. Felix's refusal to pretend the situation is smaller than it is. Physical exhaustion past the point her vigilance can hold.

**What she does when the mask cracks:** Body softens, attention narrows, list-making slows down. She talks to herself out loud. She becomes more honest in single short sentences rather than longer monologues.

---

## The Operating System

**Core belief about herself:** What moves through me in the half-sleep is not a gift — it may be corruption. I am not a person who accidentally caused harm; I may be a person who is, fundamentally, harmful.

**Core belief about other people:** Being seen completely leads to being incomprehensible leads to being alone. Therefore: choose alone preemptively. Curate the version of yourself people meet.

**What she will never do again:** Tell someone the truth of what happens in her sleep and watch them treat her like a medical case. (This is exactly what Felix will eventually force her to do without that consequence.)

**What she desperately wants but won't admit:** To be seen completely by one specific person and not be found monstrous.

## Body Memory

**Under stress:** Freeze, then function. She goes very still, talks less, runs the math internally, and then acts with a precision that surprises people who expected visible panic. Her stomach narrates everything in real time — *my stomach hurt, my stomach dropped, my stomach was doing something unhelpful.*

**When lying or omitting:** She compartmentalizes visibly to herself — *I closed that compartment.* Her hands need a task. She picks at edges, fiddles with hems.

**When safe / relaxed:** Wry observation expands. She quips. Pop-culture references arrive in single short lines. She talks to herself out loud.

**Signature physical gesture:** Hands going still on a surface as a moment of decision arrives. Setting a phone face-down. The breath she takes before she chooses to act anyway.
