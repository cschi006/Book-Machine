WHAT THIS IS

A cheat sheet for your whole Book Machine. The stage is your orchestra: each musician is one agent. Click any of them to see what they do, when to use them, and the exact words to summon them. Use it to figure out what you need next at a glance.

THE CAST

CONDUCTOR the lead session. Runs the loop, never plays or marks the score.

PLAYERS the agents — one job each, dispatched as subagents.

SECTION LEADS the Concertmaster (voice), Adjudicator (the villain critic), and Librarian (sole writer of shared state).

THE BOOTH deterministic gates. A movement that blows a budget bounces back before you ever read it.

READING THE STAGE

Sections are colour-coded by phase: Tuning Room, Rehearsal, The Booth, Principals, Dress Rehearsal, Section Leads. Everything is independently invocable — you can call one player on one chapter, or let the Conductor dispatch the whole panel.

PERFORMANCE LOG

The MY WORK tab's Performance Log is your "what has been done" list — it reads each book's state/rehearsal-log.md automatically and shows every premiere and backward wave. No manual logging; just RESCAN after Claude has worked.

CONDUCTOR — HANDING OVER THE BATON

The CONDUCTOR tab shows where a book sits in the pipeline and what’s next. RUN NEXT STEP does the single next thing; RUN NEXT BLOCK does the whole next phase. Both build a brief — COPY it into Claude Code, or WRITE TO FOLDER and say “run the brief.”

Where you make decisions: the Conductor tab’s DECISIONS panel. It reads the Ledger’s parked escalations. When Claude hits a call mid-run, it stops and asks you in the chat; longer-term calls wait for you here. BRING THESE TO CLAUDE walks you through them.

THE RULES NO AGENT BREAKS

One writer for state (the Librarian). Gates before edits, not after. Ration tics, never eliminate. Tune to the A. Commit every wave. Ten on stage max. Surface real decisions; handle the rest silently.
