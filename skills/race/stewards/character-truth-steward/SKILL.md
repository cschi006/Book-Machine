# Character-Truth Steward
**Sector:** Race · Stewards Panel · **Status:** Stub — ready to build  
**Jurisdiction:** Character behavior consistency — wound expression, mask behavior, private vocabulary, motivation coherence

---

## Purpose

The Character-Truth Steward reads against the interiority files in the Character Truth Vault. Its jurisdiction is not facts (that's the Continuity Steward) but behavior: does this character act, react, speak, and think in a way consistent with who they actually are?

The failure mode this steward catches is generic behavior — a character reacting to something the way anyone might react, rather than the way this specific person with this specific wound and this specific history would react.

The question this steward asks of every significant character moment: *Is this reaction specific, or could any character in fiction have done this?*

---

## Severity Calls

| Finding | Default severity |
|---|---|
| Behavior directly contradicts established wound/operating system | Lap invalidated |
| Generic reaction where specific history demands specific one | Pit stop |
| Mask behavior inconsistent (character reveals too much/too little for arc position) | Pit stop |
| Dialogue doesn't match character's private vocabulary | Investigation |
| Minor inconsistency in behavior that could be stress/growth | Yellow flag |

---

## Inputs

- Finalized scene draft
- Character Truth Vault files for characters in scene
- Character profiles (arc position — where are they in their wound-to-healing journey?)
- Outline (what arc position should they be at in this scene?)

---

## Process

1. For each significant character moment in the scene, pull their truth vault entry
2. Ask: given this character's wound, operating system, and mask — is this how they would actually respond?
3. Check dialogue: does each character's speech pattern, vocabulary register, and what they *don't* say match their private self?
4. Check arc position: is the character showing the right amount of wound/mask/growth for this point in the story?
5. Flag any moment that feels generic, inconsistent, or arc-position-wrong
6. Assign severity and pass to Team Principal

---
*Build notes: The prompt needs to explain how to read a truth vault entry and apply it to specific scene moments. The "generic vs. specific" distinction is the core skill to develop.*
