# Pacing Inspector
**Sector:** Parc Fermé · **Status:** Stub — ready to build  
**Role:** Compares actual scene-by-scene tension and word counts against the target tension curve across the full manuscript.

---

## Purpose

The Pacing Steward catches scene-level pacing issues during the race. The Pacing Inspector sees the whole track at once — it reads the manuscript as a tension graph and finds the drag zones, energy dips, and places where the act structure doesn't match the energy curve.

This is the altitude view the race-level steward cannot have.

---

## Inputs

- Full manuscript
- Tension curve and chapter/scene targets from Story Engineer dossier
- Race log (for any lap invalidations or structural changes made during the race)

---

## Process

1. Build actual tension map: read each scene, assign tension level (1–10 scale) and note word count
2. Plot actual curve against target curve from dossier
3. Identify drag zones: stretches where tension is flat for too long
4. Identify energy anomalies: peaks that should be valleys, valleys that should be peaks
5. Flag act structure breaks: does the act two low point land where it should?
6. Produce candidate list with location, observation, severity, and reframe suggestion
7. Author ratifies: accept / reject / revise

---

## Outputs

- Pacing map (actual vs. target)
- Flagged candidate list
- Drag zone report

---
*Build notes: Needs the dossier tension curve format. The tension-level assignment (1–10 per scene) is a judgment call that needs calibration.*
